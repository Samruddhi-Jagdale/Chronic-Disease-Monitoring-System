Emergency Warning Signs by Condition

🩸 Diabetes Emergency

Hypoglycemia (Low Sugar <70): Shakiness, sweating, confusion, unconsciousness.
Action: Consume 15g fast-acting carbs (glucose tablets, juice). Recheck in 15 min. Seek help if unconscious.

Hyperglycemia (High Sugar >250): Extreme thirst, frequent urination, blurred vision, fruity breath.
Action: Check ketones if possible. Take prescribed medication. Seek emergency care if vomiting or confusion.

💉 Hypertensive Crisis (BP >180/120)

Severe headache, vision changes, chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea.
Action: Sit down, remain calm, do NOT exercise. Call emergency services immediately. Do not take extra BP medication without doctor instructions.

❤️ Heart Attack Warning Signs

Chest pain or pressure, pain spreading to arm/jaw/back, sweating, nausea, shortness of breath, irregular heartbeat (HR >120 or <40).
Action: Call emergency services IMMEDIATELY. Chew aspirin 325mg if not allergic and no contraindication. Do not drive yourself.

🫁 Low Oxygen (<90%)

Bluish lips/fingertips (cyanosis), severe shortness of breath, rapid breathing, confusion.
Action: Sit upright, use prescribed oxygen if available. Call emergency services immediately.

🌡 High Fever (>39°C)

Chills, confusion, rash, severe headache, stiff neck, difficulty breathing.
Action: Take paracetamol if not contraindicated. Stay hydrated. Seek medical care for fever >39°C lasting more than 24 hours.
